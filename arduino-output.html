Running the Flowing LED Light Program

1 - Library Installation Steps

This flowing light experiment uses basic digital pin control and does not require any additional third-party libraries. You can write, compile, and upload the code directly in the Arduino IDE.

2.2 Code Logic Explanation

Pin Definition:

The array ledPins[] defines the 5 digital pins connected to LEDs, and numLeds stores the total number of LEDs, making it easy to perform batch operations.

setup() Initialization:

A for loop sets all LED pins to OUTPUT mode and initializes them to low level, ensuring all LEDs are off when powered on.

loop() Core Logic:

The for loop turns on each LED in sequence, keeps it on for 200ms, then turns it off. This creates a sequential lighting effect from pin 1 to pin 5, forming the "flowing light" effect.

Adjustable Parameters:

Modify the value inside delay(200) to change the flow speed of the lights. You can also change the pin numbers in the ledPins[] array to adapt to different wiring schemes.

3 - Circuit Diagram

This diagram shows the complete wiring plan for the flowing light experiment:

图片 2-2

Components

Arduino UNO main board, breadboard, 5 LED beads, 5 current-limiting resistors.

Wiring Logic

  1. Digital pins 1~5 of the Arduino are connected to the anode (long leg) of each corresponding LED through a current-limiting resistor.
  2. The cathode (short leg) of all LEDs is connected to the GND line on the breadboard.
  3. The breadboard's GND line is finally connected to the Arduino's GND pin to form a complete circuit.

4 - Physical Connection Steps

Follow these steps based on the circuit diagram:

图片 2-3

  1. Connect the Arduino UNO to your computer via a USB cable to ensure power supply and device recognition.
  2. Insert 5 LEDs into the breadboard with the anode (long leg) facing the resistor side and the cathode (short leg) facing the GND side.
  3. Connect a 220Ω current-limiting resistor to the anode of each LED, then use jumper wires to connect the resistors to Arduino digital pins 1~5.
  4. Connect the cathode of all LEDs to the Arduino's GND pin via the same column on the breadboard.
  5. Confirm there are no short circuits or loose connections before uploading the code for testing.

01. MEMO BOX

图片 2-4

Artist: He Ran
Source: Core77 Design Awards 2023

Alzheimer's disease (CTAD) is spreading at an alarming rate, seriously affecting the well-being of patients and their families. Sufferers gradually lose their memories bit by bit. Even for those who do not develop Alzheimer's, most elderly people will experience memory decline as they age.

This project aims to help seniors preserve their memories before the onset of Alzheimer's or age-related memory loss. MemoBox can record both recent events and memories from the distant past. In fact, many elderly people are eager to share their life stories with others.

Overall, MemoBox offers seniors a simple, intuitive and dignified way to document their memories. It is a product-service system consisting of a main device, guide cards, memory cards and a companion app — the app is exclusively for family members to place orders and play back audio recordings. No digital interfaces are adopted across the system except for the app, making it extremely easy for the elderly to learn and operate.

Seniors can record and share their stories via MemoBox, which brings them a strong sense of accomplishment and self-worth. When their memory fades over time, they can revisit these recorded stories to reconnect with their life and identity. Meanwhile, family members can also learn more about the elders' life experiences through MemoBox, effectively strengthening family communication and bonds.

Advantages

  1. Low learning curve & easy to use: Free of digital interfaces, it is simple and intuitive for the elderly to operate.
  2. Respectful and emotionally supportive: It records memories through story-sharing rather than medical intervention, bringing seniors a sense of achievement and self-worth.
  3. Value for multiple users: It helps elders preserve memories and allows family members to learn more about their seniors and enhance family communication.
  4. Preventive intervention: It takes effect at the early stage of Alzheimer's disease or memory decline to retain life memories.

Disadvantages

  1. Reliance on recognition accuracy: Errors in object identification or memory matching may undermine the elderly users' confidence.
  2. High long-term maintenance costs: Regular replacements of consumables like memory cards and guide cards, plus potential device malfunctions and upkeep issues.
  3. Limited functionality: It only focuses on memory recording and cannot intervene in or slow down memory loss.
  4. Privacy and data risks: Audio recordings and family sharing features on the app may lead to privacy leakage.

02. Dementia Pill Reminder Box

图片 2-5

Applicable Users

People with Alzheimer's disease, memory loss and cognitive impairment.

Functions (Well-matched to actual needs)

  • Automatically lights up and sounds a buzzer at preset times to remind users to take medicine.
  • The alert stops only when the medicine box is opened.
  • Large buttons and simple operations, easy for the elderly to use.
  • Precise timekeeping; time data won't be lost after power failure (equipped with RTC module).
  • Simple structure and low cost; can be made via 3D printing or with paper boxes.

Project Advantages

Extremely simple to operate, ideal for people with cognitive impairment. Audio and light reminders ensure medication is not missed. The alert only stops when the box is opened, preventing repeated dosing. Fully open-source, with low-cost hardware and easy to replicate. Built-in real-time clock, so time remains accurate after power loss.

Project Disadvantages

Batteries need regular replacement. No automatic network connection; time must be set manually. Functions are basic with no data recording feature.
